GreedyBot

Decision rule

Score every legal move by immediate net value. Pick the best.
Never complete a wall row (ending the game) unless currently winning.

V = S − P
SImmediate wall points gained when the pattern line fills and the tile is placed on the wall (0 if no completion).
PFloor penalty incurred this round (negative or 0). Includes penalty from the START token if taken from centre.

Priority order (tie-breaking)

  1. 1 Highest V — best immediate net gain
  2. 2 Highest pattern line — fill higher rows first (they need more tiles)
  3. 3 Avoid floor — among equal V, prefer moves with no floor penalty
  4. 4 Random — if still tied after all criteria, pick uniformly at random

PlannedBot

Decision rule

Score every legal move with a weighted utility function that looks beyond the immediate turn.
Never complete a wall row (ending the game) unless currently winning.

V = S − P + R·Wr + C·Wc + K·Wk
SImmediate wall points (same as GreedyBot).
PFloor penalty (same as GreedyBot).
RTiles in the target row after placement (0–5). Progress toward a complete row bonus (+2 pts). Weight Wr = 1.
CTiles in the target column after placement (0–5). Progress toward a complete column bonus (+7 pts). Weight Wc = 3.
KTiles of the target colour on the wall after placement (0–5). Progress toward a full colour-set bonus (+10 pts). Weight Wk = 5.

The weights (1, 3, 5) are proportional to the final bonus values (2, 7, 10). Colour-set progress is valued most because it's the hardest bonus to achieve.

Priority order (tie-breaking)

  1. 1 Highest V — maximise weighted utility
  2. 2 Finish a row — prefer moves that fill a pattern line (tile goes to wall)
  3. 3 Lowest pattern line — fill smaller rows first (they're easier to complete)
  4. 4 Deterministic — first in sorted order (no randomness)

RandomBot

Decision rule

Never complete a wall row (always filter out moves that would end the game).
Then pick uniformly at random from all remaining legal moves.

P(move) = 1 / |legal_moves|

No scoring, no utility, no tie-breaking — pure uniform random choice among safe moves.

Priority order

  1. 1 Filter — remove any move that would complete a wall row
  2. 2 Random choice — pick uniformly among what's left
